3. Layman's Explanations: Walrus and Tatum

What is Walrus?
Think of Walrus as a decentralized Dropbox or Google Drive that is custom-built for the Web3 world. Storing large files—like high-res images, videos, or massive databases—directly on a blockchain is incredibly slow and prohibitively expensive. Walrus solves this by storing the "heavy" data across a secure, decentralized network of nodes, while keeping a tiny, lightweight "receipt" on the Sui blockchain. This ensures your data is safe, cannot be secretly altered, and doesn't clog up the main network.  

What is Tatum?
Imagine you want to build an app that talks directly to the Sui blockchain. To do this from scratch, you would have to set up, run, and maintain your own "node"—a server that constantly downloads and syncs the entire history of the blockchain. This is an absolute headache. Tatum is essentially the internet provider for your app. It gives you an API key and "RPC endpoints" (which are basically just web URLs). You plug these URLs into your code, and your app can instantly read data or write transactions to the Sui blockchain without you ever having to manage the complex server infrastructure.
